I

Foundational Papers

Peer-reviewed and official-sector work from his years at the New York Fed, the IMF, and the U.S. Treasury's Office of Financial Research. 10 works

01

The Rise and Fall of the Shadow Banking System

sole author  —  Moody's Economy.com, July 2008
His first widely-cited paper. No stable public host; circulates via secondary archives.
02

Shadow Banking

with Tobias Adrian, Adam Ashcraft & Hayley Boesky  —  FRBNY Staff Report No. 458, July 2010 (revised Feb 2012)
The paper that mapped the shadow banking system and shaped the post-crisis FSB agenda.
NY Fed — overview  ·  PDF (revised)  ·  PDF (original 2010)  ·  SSRN
03

Institutional Cash Pools and the Triffin Dilemma of the U.S. Banking System

sole author  —  IMF Working Paper No. 11/190, August 2011
Citation reference — distributed via IMF.org and the SSRN author page (see Profiles).
04

Shadow Banking: Economics and Policy

with Stijn Claessens, Lev Ratnovski & Manmohan Singh  —  IMF Staff Discussion Note SDN/12/12, December 2012
The Fund's framing of shadow-banking policy; the note itself is on IMF.org.
VoxEU companion column
05

The Federal Reserve's Term Asset-Backed Securities Loan Facility (TALF)

with Adam Ashcraft & Allan Malz  —  FRBNY Economic Policy Review, Vol. 18, No. 3, November 2012
Citation reference — published in the New York Fed's Economic Policy Review.
06

Shadow Banking (journal version)

with Tobias Adrian, Adam Ashcraft & Hayley Boesky  —  FRBNY Economic Policy Review, Vol. 19, No. 2, December 2013
The peer-reviewed version of Staff Report 458.
RePEc  ·  SSRN  ·  PDF (YPFS library)
07

Shadow Banking: The Money View

sole author  —  OFR Working Paper 14-04, U.S. Treasury Office of Financial Research, July 2014
Introduces the “money view” and the hierarchy/matrix of money that underpins all his later work.
PDF (OFR)  ·  RePEc  ·  Mirror (Ex Uno Plures)
08

How the Financial System Works

sole author  —  Appendix to “Shadow Banking: The Money View,” OFR / U.S. Treasury, July 2014
The companion slide deck: the four core institutions and the dealer eco-system.
PDF (OFR)
09

An Atlas of Money Flows in the Global Financial Ecosystem

sole author  —  U.S. Treasury / Office of Financial Research, 2014
Citation reference — a dynamic map of global funding flows.
10

Bagehot was a Shadow Banker

with Perry Mehrling, James Sweeney & Daniel Neilson  —  Institute for New Economic Thinking (INET) Working Paper, November 2013
Citation reference — places shadow banking in the Bagehot / money-view tradition.
